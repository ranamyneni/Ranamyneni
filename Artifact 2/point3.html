“Then he [Woncheol] closed his eyes, twisted his mother’s scarf around Gukhwa’s neck and choked her…it was better this way, he was convinced, than to leave her afraid, slowly starving to death” (2). Woncheol saw the choice that he made to take her life as a sacrifice, one that needed to occur so that he and his brother could survive, yet her fingers were still “haunting” on his back. As the brothers continued on their journey to China after this crucial turning point, Woncheol is haunted by his little sister’s ghost.
